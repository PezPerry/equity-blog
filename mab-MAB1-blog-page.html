Business Model

Mortgage Advice Bureau (MAB) is a technology-driven UK mortgage network and broker, currently listed on AIM with a planned move to the London Stock Exchange Main Market in the second quarter of 2026. The company operates an appointed representative network model — it provides the regulatory umbrella, technology platform, compliance support, and lead generation, while individual mortgage advisers operate under the MAB brand.

Revenue comes from three main sources: mortgage procuration fees (commissions paid by lenders when a mortgage completes), protection and general insurance commissions (from selling life insurance, income protection, and buildings/contents insurance alongside the mortgage), and client fees (fees charged directly to customers for advice).

Revenue has grown uninterruptedly since 2012, with double-digit increases in all but two years (2020 and 2023). The company had 2,135 mainstream advisers at the end of FY 2025, up 10% year-on-year.

🎓 Clarity What is an appointed representative network? In the UK, anyone giving mortgage advice must be regulated by the Financial Conduct Authority. Rather than each adviser becoming individually regulated (which is expensive and complex), they can instead become an "appointed representative" of a larger firm like MAB. MAB holds the regulatory permissions and takes responsibility for compliance, while the advisers focus on advising clients. MAB earns a share of the revenue generated by each adviser.

What Matters for This Company

  • UK mortgage market volumes: MAB's revenue is directly linked to the volume of mortgage lending in the UK. Total mortgage lending was £548 billion in 2025, up 19% year-on-year. However, a lot of this was front-loaded into the first half due to the stamp duty relief deadline — a one-off tailwind that won't repeat. UK Finance forecasts just 3% growth in 2026.
  • Adviser headcount: More advisers equals more revenue capacity. The adviser base grew 10% to 2,135 — the first year of real growth in numbers since 2020. This signals improving confidence in the housing market.
  • Revenue per adviser: This measures productivity. Revenue per adviser rose 13% to £157,000, which is encouraging.
  • Market share: MAB's share of new mortgage lending was 8.4% (stable), while its share of product transfers (when customers switch to a new mortgage deal without moving house) grew from 2.7% to 3.0%.
  • Adjusted versus statutory profit gap: Adjusted profit before tax was £36.3 million versus statutory profit before tax of £22.1 million — a gap of £14.2 million. The main adjusting items include amortisation of acquired intangible assets, acquisition costs, and non-cash charges. With the company pursuing further acquisitions, these adjustments are likely to persist.

🎓 Clarity Why does mortgage market volume matter so much for MAB? MAB earns procuration fees when mortgages complete. If the total UK mortgage market grows, there are simply more mortgages being written, which means more fees for MAB — even if its market share stays flat. Conversely, when the market contracts (as it did sharply in 2023 when interest rates rose), MAB's revenue comes under pressure regardless of how well the company executes. This is why normalisation of housing volumes is critical to the investment case.

Medium-Term Targets and Long-Term Valuation

The company has set medium-term targets (announced at their 2025 Capital Markets Day) to double revenue from the FY 2024 base to £533 million, with a profit before tax margin of greater than 15%. The timeline appears to be by 2029.

I think they can probably double revenue given their track record — the compounding has been consistent. If they achieve £533 million at a 12% adjusted profit after tax margin (being slightly cautious versus their 15% profit before tax target), that would imply post-tax profit of approximately £48.0 million, giving an earnings per share of roughly 82.6p.

Risks and What Could Go Wrong

  • Mortgage market slowdown: UK Finance forecasts just 3% growth in total mortgage lending in 2026. After a stamp duty-driven first-half surge in 2025, a return to low-growth volumes would directly impact MAB's revenue.
  • Interest rate sensitivity: Higher-for-longer mortgage rates suppress housing transactions. If the Bank of England is slower to cut rates than expected, mortgage volumes could remain subdued.
  • Adjusted versus statutory profit gap persisting: With ongoing acquisition activity, amortisation of acquired intangible assets will remain a recurring cost. The £14.2 million gap between adjusted and statutory profit before tax is material.
  • Gross margin compression: Gross margin fell 190 basis points to 28.8%. If the revenue mix continues to shift towards lower-margin adviser types or product categories, margin pressure could intensify.
  • Geopolitical uncertainty: The Iran/Strait of Hormuz crisis has added volatility to mortgage markets. If this escalates, consumer confidence and housing activity could be impacted.
  • Valuation premium: The stock trades at approximately 18 times my adjusted earnings per share. For a mortgage broker — even a very good one — this is a premium valuation that leaves little room for disappointment.

What Would Change My Mind

  • Share price below 550p: At lower prices, the value opportunity would widen meaningfully, and the dividend yield would become more attractive. This is where the margin of safety would start to appear.
  • Bank of England rate cuts accelerating: A faster-than-expected rate cutting cycle would boost housing activity and mortgage volumes, directly benefiting MAB.
  • Statutory profit converging with adjusted profit: If the gap narrows — particularly if amortisation charges roll off — the earnings quality would improve significantly.
  • Revenue growth sustained above 15%: If the company can maintain double-digit revenue growth through a period of normalised (not stimulus-driven) mortgage volumes, it would support a higher growth rate assumption in my valuation.
  • Market share gains in product transfers: The improvement from 2.7% to 3.0% is encouraging. If this continues to expand, it represents a growing, less cyclical revenue stream.

Conclusion — Pass

I still really like Mortgage Advice Bureau as an operation. The business model is sound, the market position is strong, and the long-term growth record is genuinely impressive.

I just think the stock generally tends to be overpriced. At current levels, the margin of safety isn't there.

Value opportunity: −18.5% (short-term basis). Pass.
