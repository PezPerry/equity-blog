Briefing

  • Thesis: Jet2 has released a solid FY 2026 trading update. Operating profit is expected at £435–440 million, broadly in line with expectations and only marginally below the prior year's £446.5 million. The small decline is entirely attributable to £11 million of start-up and promotional costs for the new London Gatwick base. Excluding Gatwick, operating profit would have been approximately £450 million — effectively flat year-on-year.
  • Valuation view: Using estimated earnings per share of approximately 238.5p with a conservative 2% growth rate (reflecting the expected negative impact from higher costs) and a 20% crisis discount, I arrive at a valuation of approximately 1,771p. Against a current price of approximately 1,070p, this gives a value opportunity of +65.5%.
  • Key risk: The Iranian crisis has caused the booking profile to shorten, with customers booking increasingly close to departure date. This limits visibility for the peak summer period. Risk factor is elevated at 35%, and I'm only giving it a 29% probability weighting because of the external headwinds.
  • Key catalyst: London Gatwick launched 26 March 2026 with over 400,000 passengers already booked for summer. This further opens up the London and South East market. The fleet is expanding to 139 aircraft for Summer 2026.
  • Overall stance: Buy. The company is substantially undervalued at current levels.

Risks and What Could Go Wrong

  • Iranian crisis escalation: A prolonged or worsening conflict would suppress demand for Mediterranean holidays, push fuel prices higher, and further shorten the booking curve.
  • Booking curve shortening: Customers booking closer to departure reduces forward visibility and makes capacity planning harder. If this persists beyond the crisis, it becomes a structural issue.
  • Fuel price volatility: While 87% hedged for summer 2026, the remaining 13% is exposed to spot prices. FY 2027 hedging at >75% still leaves meaningful exposure.
  • Gatwick execution: The new base is performing ahead of expectations, but it is still in start-up phase. If it fails to reach profitability on the expected timeline, it will drag on group margins.
  • Inflation and consumer confidence: Higher inflation could reduce discretionary spending on holidays, particularly for the price-sensitive end of the market.
  • Low probability weighting: The 29% probability reflects the genuine uncertainty about whether the external environment allows the company to deliver on its potential.

What Would Change My Mind

  • Iranian crisis de-escalation: A resolution would immediately improve forward bookings visibility, reduce fuel price risk, and lift the 20% crisis discount from the valuation.
  • Booking curve normalising: A return to normal booking patterns would signal the geopolitical impact is fading and would support higher probability weighting.
  • Gatwick base reaching profitability: Evidence that Gatwick is contributing positively to operating profit would validate the UK network expansion strategy.
  • Share price below 900p: At lower prices, the value opportunity would widen further, making the risk/reward even more compelling despite the external headwinds.
  • FY 2027 operating profit above £475 million: Beating the prior year's £446.5 million (inclusive of Gatwick) would demonstrate genuine growth through the crisis.

Conclusion — Buy

Lots going on for Jet2 — lots to be positive about with their growth plans, operational execution, and fortress balance sheet. But also lots of external potential issues that are outside management's control.

Nonetheless, the company is substantially undervalued at current levels. The hedging provides near-term protection, the Gatwick launch extends the UK addressable market, and the integrated holiday model continues to drive customer loyalty and repeat bookings.

Value opportunity: +65.5% (probability 29%). Undervalued.
