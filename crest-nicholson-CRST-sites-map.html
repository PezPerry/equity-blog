Reading the map. Crest Nicholson’s estate is overwhelmingly a Southern and Eastern England footprint — Hampshire, Surrey, Berkshire, Sussex, Kent, Essex and the Home Counties — with a Midlands band and a small, newer Yorkshire cluster. That geography carries the highest selling prices in the country (open-market ASP of £414k against a UK new-build average nearer £300k), which makes its buyers the most exposed to mortgage affordability at a 3.75% base rate that markets now expect to stay higher for longer. Peers with heavier Northern and lower-price-point weightings have reported firmer sales rates through the same period. The Chiltern division was closed in November 2025 as part of the restructuring.
