Despite the Headwinds, easyJet Are Getting By

Briefing

  • Thesis: easyJet has released H1 FY 2026 results showing a headline loss of £552 million, 40% wider than the prior year's £394 million. That's disappointing, but revenue was up 12% to £3,954 million, passengers grew 6%, and load factor improved to 90%. The holidays business continues to be the standout — profit before tax of £61 million (up 39%) with customer growth of 22%.
  • Valuation view: Using my full-year earnings per share estimate of 61.2p (after an £80 million one-off crisis adjustment) with a 3% growth rate and a 20% valuation reduction, I arrive at a valuation of 485.68p. Against a current price of 347.45p, the value opportunity is +39.8%.
  • Key risk: They have suspended additional fuel hedging at current elevated prices — a calculated bet that the Iranian crisis resolves soon. If it doesn't, unhedged fuel exposure at $1,350/metric tonne spot versus $726 hedge price could significantly impact H2 profitability.
  • Key catalyst: easyJet holidays revenue up 30% to £518 million with expansion into German high street retailers. Net cash improved to £434 million with £4.7 billion total liquidity.
  • Overall stance: Buy. The airlines are extremely well run and managing to work around the problems adeptly.

H1 FY 2026 Results

Right off the bat, the headline loss for the first half was £552 million, as opposed to £394 million from the previous year — a 40% wider loss. That's disappointing, but there was passenger growth of 6% year-on-year and the load factor improved by 2 percentage points to 90%.

They have £4.7 billion of liquidity and net cash of £434 million, so they appear to have a good amount of cash in reserve. All in all, these figures are fine.

easyJet Holidays and Strategy

The holiday side continues to be the growth engine. They've opened up easyJet holidays in 500 high street retailers in Berlin to help accelerate growth in Germany. It's interesting that the company has done a full circle on their original plan. easyJet was set up with a phone number and early days of internet selling — the whole premise was that you didn't go via travel agents. Now they're pushing their holiday offerings into retailers.

They have also set up new bases in Marrakech and Newcastle. I would imagine these are reasonably small incremental additions to their flying bases.

🎓 Clarity Why is easyJet holidays so important to the investment case? Package holidays have much higher margins than selling flight-only tickets. When easyJet bundles a flight with hotel accommodation and transfers, the profit per customer is significantly higher than selling the seat alone. The holidays business generated £61 million profit before tax in H1 on £518 million of revenue — an 11.8% margin. The airline business, by contrast, operates on much thinner margins. As holidays grow to become a larger share of the business, the overall group margin should improve structurally.

Forward Bookings and the Iranian Conflict

A similar story to other airlines — the Iranian conflict has resulted in a later booking curve. Consumers are waiting until nearer to their holiday dates to book flights. This was a trend that was happening anyway, but bookings on the month of departure are showing year-on-year growth.

Fuel Hedging — A Calculated Bet

They do have fuel hedging in place, which has helped them avoid the cost of elevated prices. But they have suspended additional hedging because of the high fuel prices. This is a bit of a bet on the assumption that the Iranian crisis is going to stop relatively soon, and that fuel prices are going to come down from their current positions. That could come and bite them back if things go in a different direction.

I don't blame them for that. It makes a lot of sense to me. On a probability basis, you would not want to do too much fuel hedging going forwards at current elevated prices. But that is a risk.

Risks and What Could Go Wrong

  • Suspended fuel hedging: A calculated bet that fuel prices will fall. If the Iranian crisis persists or worsens, unhedged exposure at $1,350/mt spot versus $726 hedge price could materially impact profitability. Every $100/mt move costs approximately £35 million.
  • Late booking curve persisting: Consumers booking closer to departure reduces forward visibility. If this extends beyond the crisis, it becomes a structural issue for capacity planning.
  • H2 profit risk: Citi expects FY 2026 profit before tax consensus to shift from £257 million toward break-even. The range of outcomes is unusually wide.
  • Risk factor at 63%: This is a high risk factor, reflecting the genuine uncertainty about the external environment. The 44% probability weighting is correspondingly cautious.
  • Depreciation trajectory: Net depreciation up 9% to £391 million. As the fleet modernises with A321neos, depreciation charges will continue to rise and weigh on reported profits.

What Would Change My Mind

  • Iranian crisis de-escalation: Would immediately improve bookings visibility, reduce fuel price risk, and support removing the 20% valuation discount.
  • Fuel prices below $900/mt: Would allow resumption of hedging at more favourable rates and reduce the unhedged exposure risk.
  • easyJet holidays profit before tax above £150 million full-year: Would validate the holidays strategy as a genuine structural margin improver.
  • Share price below 300p: Would widen the value opportunity further and increase the dividend yield to above 4.5%.
  • Load factor sustained above 90%: Would demonstrate that underlying demand remains robust despite the geopolitical uncertainty.

Conclusion — Buy

I remain a Buy on easyJet. I ultimately think they and other airlines are extremely well run. Where they should be having much greater issues, they are managing to work around the problems adeptly.

I'm quite happy with how the company is being run, what their general strategy is on holidays, and the capacity growth at a slowish but sensible rate.

The 3.8% dividend yield is reasonably positive — that might not be especially high for a FTSE company, but I think it's okay coming from an airline at this point in time.

Buy. Value opportunity: +39.8%. Probability: 44%. Research grade: A.
